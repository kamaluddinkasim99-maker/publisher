      <!-- STATEMENT -->
      <section class="content-card" id="journal-statement">
        <div class="section-heading">
          <p>Journal Statement</p>
          <h2>Scope of the Journal</h2>
        </div>

        <p>
          Journal of Ecology and Environmental Management (JEEM) is an open access
          scholarly journal dedicated to publishing high-quality academic works in
          ecology, environmental science, environmental management, marine and
          coastal systems, conservation, sustainable resource use, and environmental
          policy. JEEM provides a platform for researchers, practitioners, policy
          analysts, and decision-makers to share scientific findings and management
          approaches that support sustainable environmental governance.
        </p>

        <p>
          JEEM welcomes manuscripts that offer original scientific contributions,
          applied research findings, conceptual development, policy analysis, and
          practical solutions related to environmental challenges. The journal
          emphasizes research that connects ecological understanding with management
          action, particularly in contexts where environmental sustainability,
          biodiversity protection, fisheries governance, climate adaptation, and
          community resilience are closely linked.
        </p>
      </section>

      <!-- ARTICLE TYPES -->
      <section class="content-card" id="article-types">
        <div class="section-heading">
          <p>Accepted Manuscripts</p>
          <h2>Types of Articles</h2>
        </div>

        <p>
          JEEM accepts several types of scholarly contributions. Submissions should
          demonstrate originality, methodological clarity, relevance to the journal
          scope, and contribution to the advancement of ecology and environmental
          management.
        </p>

        <div class="article-type-grid">
          <div>
            <h3>Original Research Article</h3>
            <p>
              Full-length papers presenting original research findings, data analysis,
              field studies, modeling, experiments, or policy-relevant evidence.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Review Article</h3>
            <p>
              Critical synthesis of current knowledge, research gaps, theoretical
              development, or future directions in ecology and environmental management.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Case Study</h3>
            <p>
              Applied studies focusing on specific locations, ecosystems, communities,
              policies, projects, or management interventions.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Short Communication</h3>
            <p>
              Concise scientific reports presenting preliminary findings, emerging
              issues, new methods, or urgent environmental observations.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Policy Brief</h3>
            <p>
              Evidence-based policy analysis or recommendations related to ecological
              sustainability, fisheries management, conservation, or environmental governance.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Technical Note</h3>
            <p>
              Notes on methods, tools, datasets, monitoring approaches, assessment
              frameworks, or practical innovations relevant to environmental management.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>

      <!-- MAIN FOCUS AREAS -->
      <section class="content-card" id="focus-areas">
        <div class="section-heading">
          <p>Research Coverage</p>
          <h2>Main Focus Areas</h2>
        </div>

        <p>
          JEEM covers a broad range of topics within ecology and environmental
          management. The journal particularly encourages interdisciplinary works
          that combine natural science, management science, policy analysis,
          technological innovation, and community-based approaches.
        </p>

        <div class="scope-cards">
          <div class="scope-card">
            <h3>Ecology and Ecosystem Studies</h3>
            <p>
              Ecosystem dynamics, biodiversity, ecological interactions, habitat
              quality, ecosystem services, ecological restoration, and environmental
              change affecting terrestrial, freshwater, coastal, and marine ecosystems.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div class="scope-card">
            <h3>Environmental Management and Governance</h3>
            <p>
              Environmental planning, institutional governance, environmental
              standards, management systems, environmental monitoring, regulatory
              frameworks, policy implementation, and sustainability assessment.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div class="scope-card">
            <h3>Marine, Coastal, and Fisheries Sustainability</h3>
            <p>
              Marine ecosystems, coastal resilience, fisheries management, stock
              assessment, catch quota policy, fishing practices, marine conservation,
              blue economy, and sustainable use of aquatic resources.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div class="scope-card">
            <h3>Pollution Control and Environmental Quality</h3>
            <p>
              Pollution sources, pollutant transport, water quality, air quality,
              soil contamination, hazardous waste, marine debris, environmental
              monitoring, mitigation technologies, and pollution control strategies.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div class="scope-card">
            <h3>Climate Change and Environmental Resilience</h3>
            <p>
              Climate adaptation, mitigation, vulnerability assessment, disaster
              risk reduction, coastal adaptation, community resilience, environmental
              risk, and nature-based solutions.
            </p>
          </div>

          <div class="scope-card">
            <h3>Conservation, Genetics, and Aquatic Forensics</h3>
            <p>
              Conservation biology, protected species management, elasmobranchii,
              aquatic biodiversity, genetic tools, species identification, trade
              traceability, and forensic approaches to support conservation policy.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>

      <!-- TARGET AUDIENCE -->
      <section class="content-card" id="target-audience">
        <div class="section-heading">
          <p>Readers and Contributors</p>
          <h2>Target Audience</h2>
        </div>

        <p>
          JEEM is intended for researchers, lecturers, graduate students, policy
          analysts, environmental managers, fisheries experts, conservation
          practitioners, government institutions, non-governmental organizations,
          professional societies, and private sector actors working on ecological
          and environmental issues.
        </p>

        <p>
          The journal aims to support the dissemination of evidence that can improve
          environmental performance, strengthen monitoring systems, guide pollution
          control and mitigation, support sustainable fisheries and marine resource
          management, and contribute to better policy design in environmental and
          ecological governance.
        </p>

        <div class="audience-grid">
          <div>
            <span>01</span>
            <h3>Academic Community</h3>
            <p>Researchers, lecturers, students, and scientific institutions.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <span>02</span>
            <h3>Policy and Government</h3>
            <p>Public agencies, policy analysts, planners, and regulators.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <span>03</span>
            <h3>Environmental Practitioners</h3>
            <p>Environmental managers, consultants, NGOs, and conservation actors.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <span>04</span>
            <h3>Marine and Fisheries Sector</h3>
            <p>Fisheries scientists, coastal managers, and blue economy stakeholders.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>

      <!-- CORE VALUES -->
      <section class="content-card" id="core-values">
        <div class="section-heading">
          <p>Journal Principles</p>
          <h2>Core Values</h2>
        </div>

        <p>
          JEEM upholds the following values in its editorial and publication process:
        </p>

        <div class="value-grid">
          <div>
            <h3>Scientific Integrity</h3>
            <p>Ensuring honesty, transparency, and responsibility in scholarly publication.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Quality</h3>
            <p>Prioritizing methodological soundness, clarity, and contribution to knowledge.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Originality</h3>
            <p>Encouraging new findings, fresh perspectives, and innovative approaches.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Clarity</h3>
            <p>Promoting clear communication of scientific evidence and policy relevance.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Innovation</h3>
            <p>Supporting new methods, tools, and interdisciplinary solutions.</p>
          </div>

          <div>
            <h3>Knowledge Dissemination</h3>
            <p>Making research useful for science, policy, management, and society.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>

      <!-- EDITORIAL RELEVANCE -->
      <section class="content-card" id="editorial-relevance">
        <div class="section-heading">
          <p>Editorial Expertise</p>
          <h2>Alignment with JEEM Editorial Board</h2>
        </div>

        <p>
          The focus and scope of JEEM are designed to reflect the expertise of its
          editorial board and the needs of contemporary environmental scholarship.
          With editorial expertise in fisheries stock assessment, sustainable
          fisheries management, blue economy policy, aquatic genetics, shark and ray
          conservation, and environmental governance, JEEM encourages manuscripts
          that are scientifically rigorous and relevant to real-world environmental
          decision-making.
        </p>

        <p>
          JEEM especially welcomes studies that bridge ecological science with
          policy and management, including research on coastal and marine ecosystems,
          aquatic biodiversity, fisheries sustainability, environmental monitoring,
          conservation technologies, and community-oriented environmental solutions.
        </p>
      </section>
